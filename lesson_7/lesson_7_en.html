AI-Powered Search Engines

Search Smarter with AI

What are they? AI search engines use smart algorithms to understand your questions and deliver accurate results.

Why they’re useful:

  • Reads Your Mind: Understands casual phrasing.
  • Tailored Results: Adapts to your search habits.
  • Next-Level Search: Use voice or images.

Try these: Google Search, Perplexity.ai, Bing

Practice: Find Answers Fast

  1. Go to Perplexity.ai.
  2. Ask, “What’s the best AI tool for students?”
  3. Notice the concise answer—summarize it in 2 sentences.
  4. Try a voice search on your phone.
Key Takeaway: AI search engines save time by cutting through the noise.

AI Writing Assistants

Write Better, Faster

What are they? AI writing tools help you write better by fixing grammar, suggesting ideas, and even drafting content.

Why they’re useful:

  • Grammar Fixer: Spots errors instantly.
  • Style Booster: Improves your writing.
  • Content Creator: Generates ideas or drafts.

Top picks: Grammarly, ChatGPT, Jasper

Practice: Upgrade Your Writing

  1. Visit Grammarly (free signup).
  2. Write a 3-sentence email about your day.
  3. Apply Grammarly’s suggestions to improve clarity.
  4. Share it with a friend (optional).

AI for Note-Taking

Organize Like a Pro

What are they? AI note-taking tools capture and organize your thoughts effortlessly.

Why they’re useful:

  • Quick Summaries: Condenses long notes.
  • Voice-to-Text: Speak, and it types.
  • Auto-Tagging: Finds notes fast.

Try these: Notion, Otter.ai, Evernote

Practice: Capture Your Ideas

  1. Sign up at Otter.ai.
  2. Record a 1-minute talk about your favorite hobby.
  3. Check the transcription—edit 1 sentence for clarity.
  4. Save it as a study note.

Practical Challenge

  1. Search “AI trends 2023” on Perplexity.ai—list 2 insights.
  2. Draft a 100-word summary with Grammarly’s help.
  3. Record your thoughts on AI with Otter.ai—summarize in 2 lines.
