What is AI?

AI Basics

Definition: Artificial Intelligence (AI) is a technology that allows computers to perform tasks that usually require human intelligence, such as learning, solving problems, or understanding language.

Key Concepts:

  • Machine Learning: Teaching computers to learn from examples, like how you learn by practicing.
  • Neural Networks: Systems inspired by the human brain to recognize patterns.
  • Natural Language Processing (NLP): Helping computers understand and talk like humans.

Brief History: AI started in the 1950s with simple programs. Today, it’s everywhere thanks to faster computers and lots of data!

Types of AI

  • Narrow AI: AI that’s good at one thing, like recognizing faces or recommending songs. Most AI today is narrow AI.
  • General AI: A future idea of AI that can do anything a human can. It doesn’t exist yet!

Hands-on Practice: Using ChatGPT

  1. Go to chat.openai.com and sign up or log in.
  2. Type a question, like “What is AI?” or “Explain photosynthesis simply.”
  3. Read the answer and ask a follow-up, like “Can you give an example?”
  4. Copy the response to your notes and tweak it to help you study.

Tips for Effective Use

  • Ask clear, specific questions for better answers.
  • Check AI responses with other sources—sometimes it’s not 100% right!
  • Use AI as a helper, not a replacement for your own thinking.

Practical Exercises

  1. Use ChatGPT to explain a topic you’re studying (e.g., “What’s the water cycle?”).
  2. Ask ChatGPT to write a short paragraph, then compare it to one you write yourself.
  3. Explore an AI tool (like Grammarly or Duolingo) and share what you learned in the course forum.
  4. Bonus: Brainstorm how AI could help with your Lesson 8 final project!
